## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, black or dark blue recommended)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side up with a corner pointing toward you (diamond shape)
2. Fold the left edge to meet the center vertical line
3. Fold the right edge to meet the center vertical line (kite base)
4. Flip the model over
5. Fold the top point down to about two-thirds of the way
6. Fold the top point back up, leaving a small portion folded down to form the head
7. Fold the entire model in half vertically (mountain fold)
8. Pull the head portion outward and crease to hold it in place
9. Fold the bottom edges inward on each side to create feet so the penguin can stand
10. Adjust the wings by slightly pulling the side flaps outward

## Tips

- **Standing balance**: Make sure the feet folds are even on both sides so the penguin stands upright
- **Head angle**: The angle of the head fold gives the penguin its character — try tilting it slightly
- **Two-tone paper**: Black-and-white duo paper creates the perfect tuxedo effect automatically

## Fun Fact

Emperor penguins are the tallest of all penguin species, standing nearly 1.2 meters tall. They endure temperatures as low as minus 60 degrees Celsius in Antarctica. Male emperor penguins famously balance their egg on their feet for over two months while the females hunt at sea.

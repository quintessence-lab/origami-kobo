## What You Need

- 1 sheet of yellow origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- 1 sheet of brown origami paper (7.5cm × 7.5cm, or cut a 15cm sheet in quarters)
- A flat surface
- Glue (optional, for securing the center)

## Instructions

1. Start with the yellow paper, white side up, and fold in half both ways to create creases
2. Fold all four corners to the center (blintz base)
3. Flip the model over and fold all four corners to the center again
4. Flip over once more and fold all four corners to the center a third time
5. Turn the model over and gently pull each corner flap outward to reveal the petals underneath
6. Continue pulling back the next layer of flaps to create a second row of petals
7. Flatten and shape all the petals evenly
8. For the center, take the brown paper and fold it into a small flat square by folding each corner to the center
9. Place the brown square in the center of the flower and secure with glue if desired

## Tips

- **Crisp folds**: Each layer of blintz folds must be neat, as errors compound over three layers
- **Petal shaping**: Take time to even out all the petals so the flower looks symmetrical
- **Size ratio**: The brown center should be about one-quarter the size of the yellow paper
- **Display**: Attach to a green paper stem and add leaves for a complete sunflower

## Fun Fact

Sunflowers exhibit a phenomenon called heliotropism — young sunflowers track the sun across the sky from east to west during the day, then reset overnight. Mature sunflowers face east permanently, which warms them in the morning and attracts more pollinators. A single sunflower head can contain up to 2,000 individual seeds arranged in a spiral pattern based on the Fibonacci sequence.

## What You Need

- 6 sheets of origami paper (6 different colors recommended, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions (One Unit)

1. Fold the paper in half both ways to create creases, then unfold

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-1.svg)

2. Fold the top and bottom edges to the center line

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-2.svg)

3. Fold the bottom-left and top-right corners into triangles

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-3.svg)

4. Tuck the triangles inward

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-4.svg)

5. Fold the top-left and bottom-right corners to the center

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-5.svg)

6. Repeat for all 6 sheets

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-6.svg)


## Assembly

1. Insert one unit's triangle into another unit's pocket

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-7.svg)

2. Connect 3 units to form one corner

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-8.svg)

3. Continue adding units one by one

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-9.svg)

4. The last unit is the trickiest — insert it carefully

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-10.svg)

5. Adjust the overall shape and you're done!

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/cube/step-11.svg)


## Tips

- **Same size**: Make sure all 6 sheets are the same size
- **Precision**: Even small differences in fold width will create gaps during assembly
- **Color placement**: Place the same color on opposite faces for a pleasing look
- **No glue needed**: With accurate folds, the cube holds together without any adhesive

## Fun Fact

Modular origami involves combining multiple identical units to create 3D shapes. The "Sonobe unit," popularized by Japanese origami artist Yukiko Sonobe, is particularly famous and beloved by origami enthusiasts around the world.

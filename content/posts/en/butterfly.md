## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Fold the paper into a triangle
2. Fold in half again to make a smaller triangle
3. Open the top layer and squash fold into a square
4. Flip and squash fold the other side (square base)
5. With the open end facing down, fold the top layer's edges to the center
6. Fold the top triangle down, crease, and unfold
7. Lift the bottom point up to create a petal fold (elongated diamond)
8. Flip and repeat on the other side
9. Turn upside down and fold the bottom point up past the top edge
10. Flip over and valley fold along the center
11. Spread the wings and adjust the shape

## Tips

- **Wing shape**: Slightly curling the wing tips adds a 3D effect
- **Patterned paper**: Floral or gradient paper makes beautiful butterflies
- **Multiple sizes**: Make several in different sizes for a stunning wall display

## Fun Fact

There are approximately 20,000 species of butterflies worldwide. In Japanese, butterflies (chō) and moths (ga) are distinguished, just as English separates "butterfly" from "moth." Butterflies are diurnal and characterized by clubbed antennae.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- A marker (optional, for drawing windows and details)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half vertically, then unfold
2. Fold the left and right edges to meet the center crease
3. Fold the entire piece in half horizontally by bringing the top edge down to the bottom
4. Fold the top layer's right edge outward to form the front of the locomotive
5. Fold the top-left corner down at a diagonal to shape the cabin roof
6. Fold the bottom-left corner up slightly and tuck it inside for the base
7. Fold a small triangle at the top-right to create the smokestack
8. Turn the model over
9. Fold the bottom edge up about 1cm to create a flat base so the train can stand
10. Draw windows, wheels, and tracks along the side with a marker

## Tips

- **Multiple cars**: Fold several trains and line them up to create a full train set
- **Smokestack**: A small reverse fold at the top corner gives a nice chimney effect
- **Color coding**: Use different colored paper for each car — blue for passenger, brown for freight
- **Display**: Glue your trains onto a strip of brown paper to create a railroad scene

## Fun Fact

Japan's Shinkansen (bullet train) was partly inspired by the kingfisher bird. Engineer Eiji Nakatsu redesigned the train's nose to mimic the bird's beak, which reduced noise when exiting tunnels and improved speed. The Shinkansen has carried over 10 billion passengers since 1964 without a single fatal accident.

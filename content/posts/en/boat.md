## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Fold the paper in half to make a rectangle (fold the top edge down to the bottom)
2. Fold in half again from left to right, crease, then unfold back to the rectangle
3. Fold the top left and right corners down to meet at the center crease
4. Fold the bottom front flap up along the base of the triangle
5. Flip over and fold the other bottom flap up the same way
6. Tuck the small corner flaps around to the other side on both ends
7. Open the bottom and flatten into a square (diamond) shape
8. Fold the front bottom corner up to the top point
9. Flip and fold the other bottom corner up to match
10. Open the bottom again and flatten into a smaller square
11. Gently pull the top two points outward to open the boat
12. Flatten and shape the bottom so the boat can stand on its own

## Tips

- **Firm creases**: Every fold needs to be sharp and precise for the final opening to work smoothly
- **Opening gently**: The last step requires patience — pull slowly and evenly on both sides
- **Waterproofing**: Use foil-backed paper or wax paper if you want the boat to float for longer
- **Sizing**: Larger paper makes the opening step much easier for beginners

## Fun Fact

The origami boat is one of the oldest known paper folds, with instructions appearing in European texts as early as the 1400s. In many countries, children fold paper boats and race them in rain puddles and streams. This design is special because it can actually float — making it one of the few origami models with a practical function beyond decoration.

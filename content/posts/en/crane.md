## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up and fold diagonally to make a crease, then unfold
2. Fold along the other diagonal and unfold
3. Fold in half horizontally and vertically to create creases
4. Collapse along the creases into a square base
5. Fold the top two flaps to meet the center crease
6. Fold the top triangle down, crease, and unfold
7. Lift the bottom point up, folding the sides inward to create a petal fold
8. Flip and repeat steps 5-7 on the other side
9. Fold the left and right flaps to the center (both sides)
10. Pull one narrow point up and to the side to form the neck
11. Pull the other point up to form the tail
12. Fold the tip of the neck down to create the head
13. Gently spread the wings apart

## Tips

- **Sharp creases**: Press each fold firmly with your nail for a clean result
- **Paper weight**: Thin paper (like washi) is easier to fold
- **Opening wings**: Hold the body from the bottom while gently pulling the wings apart

## Fun Fact

In Japan, cranes symbolize longevity and happiness. Legend has it that folding 1,000 cranes (senbazuru) will grant your wish. The tradition of gifting senbazuru for recovery from illness or at weddings continues to this day.

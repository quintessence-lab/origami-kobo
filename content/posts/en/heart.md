## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half vertically, then unfold
2. Fold the paper in half horizontally, then unfold
3. Fold the top edge down to the center crease
4. Flip the paper over
5. Fold the top-left and top-right corners down to meet the center crease, forming a triangle at the top
6. Flip the paper over again
7. Fold the left and right sides inward to meet the center crease
8. Fold the bottom point up to meet the top edge
9. Fold the bottom-left and bottom-right corners of the lower section inward at a diagonal
10. Flip the paper over
11. Fold the two small top corners and the two side points inward slightly to round out the heart shape
12. Flip the paper over to see the finished heart

## Tips

- **Paper color**: Red or pink paper is classic, but metallic gold or silver looks elegant
- **Write a message**: Before folding, write a note on the white side — it will be hidden inside
- **Symmetry**: Take extra care that both sides match for a balanced heart shape
- **Attach to cards**: Use a small piece of double-sided tape to attach the heart to a greeting card

## Fun Fact

The heart symbol we know today doesn't actually look like a real human heart. Historians believe the shape may have originated from the seed of the silphium plant, which was used in ancient Rome and was so valuable it appeared on coins. Origami hearts have become one of the most popular folds for Valentine's Day around the world.

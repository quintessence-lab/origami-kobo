## What You Need

- 2 sheets of origami paper (15cm × 15cm each, same color recommended)
- A flat surface
- Tape or glue (to join the two halves)

## Instructions

1. Take the first sheet and place it colored side down

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-1.svg)

2. Fold it in half horizontally, then unfold

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-2.svg)

3. Fold the top and bottom edges to the center crease

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-3.svg)

4. Fold in half vertically, then unfold

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-4.svg)

5. Fold all four corners inward to meet the nearest crease lines, forming triangular points

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-5.svg)

6. Fold the left half behind the right half along the center crease

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-6.svg)

7. Repeat steps 1-6 with the second sheet to create an identical unit

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-7.svg)

8. Open each unit into a band shape with triangular points sticking up

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-8.svg)

9. Slide one unit inside the other, overlapping at the ends

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-9.svg)

10. Secure the overlapping sections with a small piece of tape or glue

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-10.svg)

11. Adjust the crown into a circular shape

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-11.svg)

12. Bend the triangular points outward slightly for a regal look

![Step 12](/images/origami/steps/crown/step-12.svg)


## Tips

- **Sizing**: For a child's head, two 15cm sheets work well. For adults, use larger paper or add a third unit
- **Sturdiness**: Cardstock or thick origami paper makes a crown that holds its shape better
- **Decoration**: Add sticker gems, glitter, or draw jewels on the points
- **Party activity**: Pre-fold the units and let kids assemble and decorate their own crowns

## Fun Fact

Crowns have been symbols of authority for over 5,000 years. The earliest known crowns were found in ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia. In Japan, the traditional imperial crown is called a "kanmuri" and has a completely different shape from Western crowns. This origami crown design is popular at birthday parties and children's events throughout the world.

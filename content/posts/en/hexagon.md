## What You Need

- 3 sheets of origami paper (different colors recommended, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Take the first sheet and fold it in half lengthwise to create a long rectangle
2. Fold in half lengthwise again to make a narrow strip
3. Repeat steps 1-2 with the other two sheets so you have three strips
4. Fold each strip into a zigzag: fold the left end up at a 60-degree angle, then fold it back down to create a Z shape
5. Each strip should now form a parallelogram shape
6. Take the first strip and lay it flat in a V shape
7. Weave the second strip through the first, offsetting it by 60 degrees
8. Weave the third strip through both, tucking the ends under and over alternately
9. Tighten the weave by gently pulling the strips snug
10. Tuck all loose ends into the pockets formed by the neighboring strips
11. Flatten the coaster and press firmly to set the shape

## Tips

- **Consistent width**: Make sure all three strips are folded to the same width
- **Angle precision**: The 60-degree folds are key — use a protractor if needed on your first try
- **Tight weave**: A loose weave will fall apart; keep tension as you assemble
- **Durability**: For a sturdier coaster, use thicker paper or cardstock

## Fun Fact

Hexagons are one of nature's favorite shapes. Honeybees build hexagonal cells because the shape tiles perfectly with no gaps and uses the least amount of wax for the most storage space. Engineers call this "hexagonal efficiency."

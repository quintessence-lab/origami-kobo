## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm for display, or newspaper for a wearable version)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half diagonally to make a triangle with the point at the top
2. Fold the left and right corners up to meet the top point, forming a diamond shape
3. Fold those same two flaps down and outward — the tips should point to the left and right like horns
4. Take the front layer of the bottom section and fold it up to the base of the triangle, stopping at the horizontal center line
5. Fold that same flap up one more time to create the brim of the helmet
6. Tuck the remaining back layer of paper behind the helmet or fold it up inside

## Tips

- **Wearable size**: Use a full sheet of newspaper (about 54cm × 40cm) to make a helmet large enough for a child to wear
- **Crisp folds**: The horn folds in step 3 define the helmet's character — angle them symmetrically
- **Decoration**: Draw a crest (mon) on the front brim with a gold marker for an authentic samurai look
- **Sturdy brim**: Fold the brim carefully in two even layers so it lies flat and holds its shape

## Fun Fact

The kabuto was an essential part of a samurai's armor, designed not only for protection but also to intimidate enemies. Real kabuto often featured elaborate crests called maedate — from crescent moons to antlers to mythical creatures. Folding paper kabuto on Children's Day (May 5th) symbolizes the wish for children to grow up strong and courageous.

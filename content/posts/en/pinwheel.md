## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A straw or thin wooden stick
- A pushpin or thumbtack
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half to form a rectangle, then unfold
2. Fold in half the other way to create a cross-shaped crease, then unfold
3. Cut along each crease line from the edge toward the center, stopping about 2cm from the middle
4. You should now have four triangular flaps
5. Take the right corner of each flap and fold it toward the center — do not fold the left corners
6. Overlap all four folded corners at the center point
7. Push a pin through all the layered corners and the center of the paper
8. Attach the pin to the top of a straw or stick, leaving a small gap so it can spin freely

## Tips

- **Even cuts**: Make sure your four cuts are the same length for a balanced pinwheel
- **Loose pin**: The pinwheel needs room to rotate, so do not push the pin in too tightly
- **Stiff paper**: Slightly thicker paper or double-sided colored paper holds its shape better in the wind
- **Two-tone effect**: Use paper with different colors on each side for a striking spinning pattern

## Fun Fact

Pinwheels (kazaguruma) have been popular toys in Japan since the Edo period. They were originally sold at temples and shrines during New Year celebrations as good luck charms, symbolizing the turning of fortune in one's favor.

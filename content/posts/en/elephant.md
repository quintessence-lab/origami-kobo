## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, gray recommended)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up with a corner pointing toward you (diamond shape)

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-1.svg)

2. Fold the left and right edges to meet the center line (kite base)

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-2.svg)

3. Flip the model over

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-3.svg)

4. Fold the left and right edges to the center line again

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-4.svg)

5. Fold the model in half horizontally, bringing the top point down to meet the bottom

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-5.svg)

6. Fold the top point back up, leaving a small section folded to form the trunk

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-6.svg)

7. Fold the trunk in a zigzag pattern (accordion fold) to give it a curved, realistic shape

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-7.svg)

8. Fold the model in half vertically

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-8.svg)

9. On each side, pull the large flap outward and crease to form the ears

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-9.svg)

10. Fold the back end inward with a reverse fold to create the tail

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-10.svg)

11. Open the bottom slightly so the elephant can stand

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/elephant/step-11.svg)


## Tips

- **Trunk shape**: The zigzag fold in step 7 is the trickiest part — take your time and make precise creases
- **Big ears**: Pull the ear flaps out generously for a true elephant look
- **Standing**: Adjust the bottom folds until the elephant balances on its own
- **Paper size**: Using larger paper (20cm+) makes the trunk folds much easier

## Fun Fact

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, with African elephants weighing up to 6,000 kilograms. They are known for their remarkable memory and intelligence. Elephants communicate using low-frequency sounds called infrasound, which can travel several kilometers through the ground.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, white or pink recommended)
- A flat surface
- A marker (optional, for drawing the face)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up and fold it diagonally to form a triangle

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-1.svg)

2. Position the triangle with the long edge at the bottom

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-2.svg)

3. Fold the bottom edge up about 1cm to create a small hem

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-3.svg)

4. Fold the left corner up toward the top point at an angle

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-4.svg)

5. Fold the right corner up toward the top point at the same angle, forming the two ears

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-5.svg)

6. Flip the model over

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-6.svg)

7. Fold the bottom point up to shape the chin

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-7.svg)

8. Fold the left and right corners inward slightly to round the face

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-8.svg)

9. Flip back to the front

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-9.svg)

10. Draw eyes, a small triangular nose, and a mouth

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/rabbit/step-10.svg)


## Tips

- **Ear length**: Adjust how far up you fold the ears in steps 4-5 to make them longer or shorter
- **Round face**: The small folds in step 8 are key to giving the rabbit a soft, round appearance
- **Seasonal display**: Pair with origami eggs or flowers for a spring-themed arrangement

## Fun Fact

In Japanese folklore, rabbits are said to live on the moon, where they pound mochi (rice cakes) with a mortar and pestle. This legend comes from the pattern of dark spots visible on the moon's surface, which Japanese people have traditionally seen as the shape of a rabbit.

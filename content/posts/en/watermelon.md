## What You Need

- 1 sheet of duo origami paper (red/green, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- Black marker (for drawing seeds)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper with the green side up and fold it in half to make a rectangle
2. Fold the rectangle in half again to form a square
3. Unfold the last fold, returning to the rectangle shape
4. Fold the top left and right corners down to the center bottom edge
5. Fold the bottom edge up about 1cm to create the rind
6. Flip the model over to see the red side showing through as the fruit flesh
7. Fold the left and right points slightly inward to round the slice shape

## Finishing Touch

Use a black marker to draw small teardrop-shaped seeds on the red surface.

## Tips

- **Duo paper**: Red/green duo paper is perfect; if unavailable, glue a red and green sheet together
- **Rind width**: Adjust the fold in step 5 to make the green rind thicker or thinner
- **Seeds**: Draw seeds in a scattered pattern following the curve for a realistic look
- **Size**: Use large paper (20cm+) to make a decorative piece for summer displays

## Fun Fact

Watermelons are over 90% water, which is how they got their name. In Japan, a popular summer game called "suikawari" involves blindfolded players trying to smash a watermelon with a stick — similar to hitting a pinata. Japan is also known for growing square watermelons by placing them in box-shaped molds as they grow.

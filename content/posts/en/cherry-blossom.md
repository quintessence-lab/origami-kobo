## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, pink or light pink recommended)
- Scissors
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half to form a rectangle
2. Fold the rectangle in half again to mark the center, then unfold
3. Take the bottom-right corner and fold it up to meet the center mark on the top edge
4. Take the bottom-left corner and fold it over to align with the right edge of the previous fold
5. Fold the top-left section behind along the edge of the front flap
6. You now have a wedge shape — this represents one-fifth of the circle
7. Draw a petal shape on the top edge: a gentle curve that dips in at the center of each petal
8. Cut along the line with scissors
9. Carefully unfold the paper to reveal a five-petaled cherry blossom
10. Gently curl each petal inward slightly for a three-dimensional effect
11. Use a marker to add small lines from the center for the stamens (optional)

## Tips

- **Petal notch**: The small V-shaped cut at the center of each petal is what distinguishes cherry blossoms from other flowers
- **Paper weight**: Thinner paper unfolds more easily and gives a more delicate appearance
- **Multiple blooms**: Make several in slightly different pink shades for a realistic branch display
- **No scissors version**: You can also fold a five-petal flower without cutting by using five small squares joined together

## Fun Fact

Cherry blossoms (sakura) are Japan's unofficial national flower and bloom for only about one to two weeks each spring. The tradition of hanami — gathering under the blossoming trees to enjoy food and drink with friends — dates back over a thousand years to the Nara period. The Japan Meteorological Corporation even tracks the "cherry blossom front" as it moves northward across the country each spring.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (any color, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- Markers (optional, for details)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper color side down and fold it in half vertically, then unfold
2. Fold the top left and right corners down to meet at the center crease
3. Fold the left and right edges to the center crease (creating a narrow kite shape)
4. Fold the model in half along the center crease (the color should be on the outside)
5. Rotate the model so the pointed end faces upward
6. Fold one side flap down and outward at an angle to create a fin
7. Flip and fold the other flap to match, creating the second fin
8. Open the body slightly so the rocket can stand upright between its fins
9. Adjust the nose cone point and fins until the rocket stands balanced

## Tips

- **Sharp nose**: Make the folds at the top extra crisp for a clean, pointed nose cone
- **Fin angle**: Adjust the angle of the fins to help the rocket stand on its own
- **Decoration**: Draw a window (porthole), a flag, or panel lines with markers
- **Flames**: Cut small strips of red, orange, and yellow paper and glue them to the bottom for a launch effect

## Fun Fact

The first liquid-fueled rocket was launched by Robert Goddard in 1926 — it flew for just 2.5 seconds and reached a height of only 12.5 meters. Today, rockets like SpaceX's Falcon 9 can reach speeds of over 27,000 km/h. Japan's space agency JAXA operates the Tanegashima Space Center, one of the most scenic launch sites in the world, located on a tropical island in southern Japan.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (purple, blue, or pink, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- Scissors (optional, for petal shaping)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper color side down and fold diagonally to make a triangle
2. Fold the triangle in half again to make a smaller triangle
3. Lift the top flap and open it, squashing it down into a square (squash fold)
4. Flip over and repeat the squash fold on the other side
5. Fold the left and right edges of the top layer to the center crease
6. Fold the top triangle down and crease firmly
7. Unfold steps 5-6, then lift the bottom point and petal fold upward
8. Flip and repeat steps 5-7 on the other side
9. Fold the top flaps down on each side to open up the flower shape
10. Gently open the petals outward and curl them slightly with your finger

## Tips

- **Petal curling**: Wrap each petal around a pencil to give them a natural curve
- **Color choice**: Deep purple and blue create the most realistic look
- **Display idea**: Attach to a green pipe cleaner or twisted paper stem for a climbing vine effect
- **Size variation**: Make several in different sizes for a more natural arrangement

## Fun Fact

Morning glories are one of the most beloved summer flowers in Japan, where they are called "asagao" (morning face). Japanese elementary school students traditionally grow morning glories as their first gardening project. The flowers bloom at dawn and close by afternoon, giving them their poetic name.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half horizontally, then unfold
2. Fold the bottom-left corner up to the center of the top edge
3. Unfold, then fold the top-left corner down to meet the crease you just made
4. Fold the bottom-left corner up again so it meets the right edge of the triangle from step 3
5. Fold the right side of the paper behind along the edge of the layered triangle
6. Cut along the top diagonal edge through all layers to create a pentagon shape (or fold and tear carefully)
7. Unfold the pentagon completely
8. Fold the pentagon in half through each point to its opposite edge, creating 5 creases, then unfold each time
9. Fold the pentagon in half connecting each pair of adjacent points, creating 5 more creases
10. Using the mountain and valley folds, collapse the pentagon into a flat five-pointed star shape
11. Tuck the overlapping flaps underneath to lock the star together
12. Sharpen all the points and edges for a crisp finish

## Tips

- **Pentagon precision**: The star's beauty depends on having a good pentagon — take your time with steps 1-6
- **Crease direction**: Mountain and valley folds must alternate correctly for the collapse to work
- **Puffed star**: Push the center gently from behind to create a 3D puffed star effect
- **Paper type**: Foil-backed origami paper makes stars that catch the light beautifully

## Fun Fact

The five-pointed star is one of the oldest symbols in human history, appearing in Sumerian writing around 3000 BCE. In Japan, the five-pointed star (called "goboshi") is associated with the legendary onmyoji Abe no Seimei and is used as a protective talisman. Origami stars are popular Christmas and Tanabata (Star Festival) decorations.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of duo origami paper (red/white or brown/white, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- White circle stickers or white marker (optional)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up and fold it in half to make a rectangle
2. Unfold, then fold both sides to the center crease
3. Unfold everything and fold the paper in half the other way (top to bottom)
4. Fold the top left and right corners down to the center line to form a triangle shape at the top
5. Fold the bottom left and right edges inward slightly to narrow the stem
6. Fold the bottom edge up about 1cm to create a flat base
7. Flip the model over — the colored top is the mushroom cap, and the white bottom is the stem
8. Fold the top left and right corners back slightly to round out the cap

## Finishing Touch

Add white circle stickers or draw white dots on the cap for a classic toadstool look. You can also draw a cute face on the stem.

## Tips

- **Cap color**: Red makes a fairy-tale toadstool; brown creates a realistic shiitake look
- **Duo paper**: Red/white duo paper gives the best result with no extra steps
- **Proportion**: Adjust how much you fold in step 4 to change the cap-to-stem ratio
- **Faces**: Drawing a simple smiley face on the stem makes it extra charming

## Fun Fact

There are over 10,000 known species of mushrooms worldwide. In Japan, mushroom hunting in autumn is a beloved tradition, especially for prized matsutake mushrooms, which can sell for hundreds of dollars per kilogram. The largest living organism on Earth is actually a honey fungus in Oregon, USA, spanning over 9 square kilometers underground.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (white or light blue, 15cm × 15cm)
- Scissors
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half to form a rectangle
2. Fold the rectangle in half again to mark the center of the top edge, then unfold back to the rectangle
3. Bring the bottom-right corner up to the left side, aligning it so the fold passes through the bottom-right of the rectangle — this creates a 60-degree angle
4. Fold the left section over to the right, matching the edges
5. You should now have a wedge shape — this is one-twelfth of the full circle
6. Fold the wedge in half one more time
7. Cut off the uneven top at an angle to form a pointed tip
8. While still folded, cut small triangles, notches, and curves along both edges
9. Be creative with your cuts — every pattern will be unique
10. Carefully unfold the paper to reveal your six-pointed snowflake

## Tips

- **Thin paper**: Thinner paper is easier to cut through the many layers
- **Sharp scissors**: Small, pointed scissors give you the most control for detailed cuts
- **Gentle unfolding**: Unfold slowly and carefully to avoid tearing the delicate parts
- **Symmetry check**: Fold precisely at each step — even small errors get magnified sixfold

## Fun Fact

No two snowflakes are exactly alike — or so the saying goes. Wilson Bentley, a Vermont farmer, spent over 40 years photographing more than 5,000 individual snowflakes starting in 1885. He never found two identical ones, earning him the nickname "Snowflake" Bentley.

## What You Need

- 2 sheets of origami paper (different colors, 15cm × 15cm)
- Glue stick
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Cut each sheet into strips about 2.5cm wide and 15cm long (you need 2 strips)
2. Glue the end of one strip perpendicular to the end of the other strip, forming an L shape
3. Fold the bottom strip over the top strip
4. Fold the strip that is now on the bottom over the top strip
5. Continue alternating folds — always folding the bottom strip over the top
6. Keep folding until you reach the end of both strips
7. Glue the final ends together
8. Let the glue dry, then gently pull the two ends apart
9. The spring will expand into a zigzag coil that bounces back when released

## Tips

- **Strip length**: For a longer spring, glue additional strips end to end before you start folding
- **Even width**: Keep the strip widths consistent for a uniform spring
- **Tight folds**: Press each fold firmly and keep the edges aligned
- **Contrasting colors**: Two different colors create a pleasing alternating pattern

## Fun Fact

This paper spring works on the same principle as a real metal spring — it stores potential energy when compressed or stretched. The Japanese call this type of craft "paper engineering" (kami no kougaku), where simple folds create surprisingly functional mechanisms.

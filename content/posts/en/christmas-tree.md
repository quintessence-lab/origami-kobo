## What You Need

- 3 sheets of green origami paper (15cm, 12cm, and 9cm squares)
- 1 small sheet of yellow or gold paper (for the star)
- A flat surface

## Instructions (Tree Layers)

1. Take the largest green sheet and place it colored side down
2. Fold diagonally both ways to create an X crease, then unfold
3. Fold in half horizontally and vertically, then unfold
4. Collapse into a preliminary base (square base)
5. Fold the left and right edges of the front flap to the center line
6. Flip and repeat on the back
7. Fold the bottom point upward to create a flat base so the layer can stand
8. Repeat steps 1-7 with the medium and small sheets

## Assembly

1. Stack the three layers from largest at the bottom to smallest on top
2. You can insert a toothpick through the center to hold them together
3. Fold a small star from the yellow paper and place it on the very top

## Star (Simple Method)

1. Cut a small square of yellow paper (about 5cm × 5cm)
2. Fold it in half, then into thirds to create wedge-shaped creases
3. Cut a zigzag line across the folded paper
4. Unfold to reveal a star shape

## Tips

- **Graduated sizes**: Use three clearly different paper sizes for a natural tree silhouette
- **Trunk**: Roll a small piece of brown paper into a cylinder and attach it to the bottom
- **Ornaments**: Add tiny dots of color with a marker or glue on small paper circles
- **Forest display**: Make several trees in different sizes for a miniature winter forest

## Fun Fact

The tradition of decorating Christmas trees originated in 16th-century Germany. Martin Luther is said to have been the first to add lighted candles to a tree, inspired by the beauty of stars twinkling through evergreen branches on a winter night.

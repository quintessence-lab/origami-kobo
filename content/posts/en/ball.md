## What You Need

- 12 sheets of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, 2-3 colors recommended)
- Glue (optional, for a sturdier finish)
- String (for hanging)
- A flat surface

## Instructions (One Flower Unit)

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold diagonally to make a triangle
2. Fold the left and right corners up to meet the top point, forming a square shape
3. Fold each of those flaps in half outward, opening them into small kite shapes
4. Open each kite flap and squash fold it flat
5. Fold the top triangles of the squashed flaps downward so they are flush with the edge
6. Refold the kite shapes in half, tucking the triangles inside
7. Apply glue to one outer face and bring the two sides together to form a cone-shaped petal
8. Repeat to make 12 flower units in total

## Assembly

1. Glue 3 flower units together side by side to form a half-sphere cluster
2. Make a second cluster of 3 units
3. Continue making clusters until you have 4 groups of 3 flowers
4. Glue two clusters together to form a half ball
5. Attach the remaining two clusters to complete the sphere
6. Thread a string through the top for hanging

## Tips

- **Patience is key**: Take your time with each unit — consistency matters more than speed
- **Color planning**: Arrange colors symmetrically for the most attractive result
- **Size matters**: Smaller paper (7.5cm) creates a delicate ball; larger paper is easier to fold
- **Let glue dry**: Wait for each section to dry before adding the next to keep the shape clean

## Fun Fact

Kusudama means "medicine ball" in Japanese. In ancient times, these decorative balls were filled with herbs and incense and hung above doorways to ward off evil spirits and disease. Today they are purely decorative, but the name endures as a reminder of their medicinal origins.

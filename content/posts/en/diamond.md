## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, metallic or glitter paper recommended)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold diagonally both ways to create an X crease, then unfold
2. Fold in half horizontally and vertically to create a plus-shaped crease, then unfold
3. Collapse the paper into a preliminary base (square base) using the creases
4. With the open end pointing down, fold the left and right edges of the top layer to the center line
5. Flip and repeat on the other side
6. Fold the top triangle down on both sides to create a crease, then unfold
7. Perform a petal fold on both sides by lifting the bottom point upward
8. Fold the left and right flaps of each side to the center line again
9. You should now have a narrow kite shape with four flaps
10. Fold the bottom point of each flap upward at a slight angle to form the facets of the diamond
11. Gently open the model from the bottom, shaping it into a 3D diamond form
12. Pinch the edges to define the facets and adjust the overall shape

## Tips

- **Foil paper**: Metallic origami paper gives the most realistic diamond appearance
- **Symmetry matters**: Keep your folds perfectly centered to get an even diamond shape
- **Gentle shaping**: When opening the 3D form, work slowly to avoid tearing
- **Size variation**: Try different paper sizes to create a set of diamonds for a mobile

## Fun Fact

The word "diamond" comes from the Greek word "adamas," meaning unconquerable. While real diamonds are the hardest natural material on Earth, your paper diamond is a lot easier to make — and costs considerably less!

## What You Need

- 2 sheets of origami paper (1 for the cake body, 1 for the frosting — different colors, 15cm × 15cm)
- 1 small piece of paper (for the candle)
- Glue stick
- A flat surface
- Markers (for decoration)

## Instructions (Cake Body)

1. Place the paper color side down and fold it in half to make a rectangle

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-1.svg)

2. Fold in half again to make a square, then unfold back to the rectangle

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-2.svg)

3. Fold the top and bottom edges to the center crease

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-3.svg)

4. Fold the left and right edges to the center crease

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-4.svg)

5. Unfold the left and right folds, then tuck the corners inward using the creases to form a box-like shape

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-5.svg)

6. Flatten and adjust to create a rectangular cake base

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-6.svg)

7. Fold the bottom edge up about 1cm for a flat base

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-7.svg)


## Instructions (Frosting)

1. Fold the second sheet in half to make a rectangle

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-8.svg)

2. Cut or fold a wavy line along the bottom edge to create a dripping frosting effect

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-9.svg)

3. Fold the sides to match the width of the cake body

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-10.svg)

4. Attach with glue on top of the cake body

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-11.svg)


## Adding the Candle

1. Roll the small piece of paper into a thin tube and glue it closed

![Step 12](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-12.svg)

2. Cut a small flame shape from yellow or orange paper

![Step 13](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-13.svg)

3. Glue the flame to the top of the candle, then glue the candle to the top of the cake

![Step 14](/images/origami/steps/cake/step-14.svg)


## Tips

- **Color pairing**: Pink frosting on a brown cake, or white frosting on a pastel cake look great
- **Decoration**: Draw strawberries, chocolate chips, or cream rosettes with markers
- **Layers**: Stack two cake bodies for a two-tier cake
- **Birthday card**: Glue the finished cake onto a folded card for a handmade birthday greeting

## Fun Fact

The tradition of putting candles on birthday cakes dates back to ancient Greece, where round cakes with candles were offered to Artemis, the goddess of the moon. The candles represented moonlight. The modern custom of blowing out candles and making a wish became popular in 18th-century Germany, where a single large candle called the "light of life" was placed on the cake.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (orange, 15cm × 15cm)
- Black marker (for the face)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up and fold it in half diagonally to make a triangle

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-1.svg)

2. Fold the triangle in half again to mark the center, then unfold back to the large triangle

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-2.svg)

3. Fold the left and right corners upward toward the top point, slightly past the center line

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-3.svg)

4. Flip the paper over

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-4.svg)

5. Fold the top point down about one-third of the way — this forms the stem area

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-5.svg)

6. Fold the very tip back up slightly to create the pumpkin stem

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-6.svg)

7. Fold the left and right corners inward at an angle to round out the pumpkin shape

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-7.svg)

8. Fold the bottom point up slightly to flatten the base

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-8.svg)

9. Flip back over to the front — you should see a pumpkin shape

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/jack-o-lantern/step-9.svg)


## Finishing Touch

Draw a jack-o'-lantern face with a black marker. Classic triangle eyes and a jagged grin work great, or get creative with your own spooky expression!

## Tips

- **Double-sided paper**: Orange on one side, green on the other makes a natural-looking stem
- **Multiple sizes**: Make several in different sizes for a pumpkin patch display
- **Expressions**: Try different faces — happy, scary, surprised — for variety
- **Wall display**: Tape a row of jack-o'-lanterns along a string for a Halloween garland

## Fun Fact

The jack-o'-lantern tradition comes from an Irish legend about "Stingy Jack," who tricked the devil and was doomed to wander the Earth with only a carved turnip to light his way. When Irish immigrants came to America, they discovered that pumpkins were much easier to carve than turnips!

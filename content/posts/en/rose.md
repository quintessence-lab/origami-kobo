## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (red or pink, 15cm × 15cm or larger recommended)
- A flat surface
- Tweezers (helpful but optional)

## Instructions

1. Create a grid of creases by folding the paper into quarters both ways (16 squares)
2. Add diagonal creases as well
3. Using the center 4 squares, collapse into a small square
4. Rotate each edge of the square slightly to create a spiral
5. Lift each of the 4 corners outward and curl them (first layer of petals)
6. Pull out more paper from the outside to form the second layer of petals
7. Shape the bottom so the rose can stand on its own
8. Curl the petal tips using tweezers or a pen

## Tips

- **Paper size**: Practice with 20cm+ paper for easier folding
- **The spiral**: The initial twist is the key to success — go slowly
- **Curling petals**: Wrap tips around a pen for natural-looking curls
- **Practice**: Start with newspaper or larger sheets before using origami paper

## Fun Fact

This design is based on the "Kawasaki Rose" created by mathematician Toshikazu Kawasaki. It's a world-famous example of the intersection of mathematics and origami, where precise geometric crease patterns transform into a beautiful flower.

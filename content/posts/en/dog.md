## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- A marker (optional, for drawing the face)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up and fold it diagonally to form a triangle
2. Position the triangle with the long edge at the top
3. Fold the left and right corners of the top edge downward at an angle to create floppy ears
4. Fold the bottom point of the front layer upward slightly to form the chin
5. Fold a small portion of the chin back down to shape the nose area
6. Flip the model over
7. Fold the bottom point up to create the back of the head
8. Flip back to the front and draw eyes and a nose with a marker

## Tips

- **Ear angle**: Adjusting the angle of the ear folds changes the breed — wider for a beagle, narrower for a collie
- **Paper color**: Brown, tan, or white paper works best for a realistic look
- **Face details**: Use a black marker for the nose and round dots for the eyes to give your dog personality

## Fun Fact

Dogs were one of the first animals depicted in origami. The simple dog face is often the very first model taught to children in Japan, making it a gateway into the world of paper folding. In Japanese, "inu" (dog) is one of the twelve animals of the zodiac.

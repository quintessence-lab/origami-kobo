## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, any color)
- Marker or stickers (for eyes and scales)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side up and fold it in half lengthwise to create a rectangle
2. Unfold and fold both long edges to the center crease
3. Fold the paper in half along the original center crease (the colored side should be on the outside)
4. At one end, fold the top and bottom corners outward at an angle to form the tail fin — the folds should fan out like a V
5. At the other end, fold a small triangle inward on the top and bottom layers to round the mouth
6. Open the body slightly so the koinobori has a three-dimensional shape

## Finishing Touch

Draw a large round eye near the mouth end. Add curved lines or small U-shapes along the body to represent scales. Use different colors for each fish to make a family set.

## Tips

- **Color choices**: Traditionally, the largest carp is black (father), followed by red (mother), and smaller ones in blue or green (children)
- **Size variation**: Use 15cm, 12cm, and 9cm paper to create a family of different sizes
- **Display**: Attach the fish to a stick or string and hang them where they can catch the breeze
- **Scale details**: Small stickers or stamped circles make great fish scales

## Fun Fact

Koinobori are flown across Japan on Children's Day (May 5th) to wish children health and success. The carp was chosen because of a Chinese legend in which a carp swam upstream and leaped over a waterfall to become a dragon — symbolizing determination and courage.

## What You Need

- 2 sheets of origami paper (pink or white, 15cm × 15cm)
- 1 sheet of origami paper (green, 15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Take one pink sheet and fold it in half both ways, then unfold to create a cross crease

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-1.svg)

2. Fold all four corners to the center point

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-2.svg)

3. Fold all four new corners to the center again

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-3.svg)

4. Fold the corners to the center one more time (three layers total)

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-4.svg)

5. Flip the model over carefully

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-5.svg)

6. Fold all four corners to the center on this side as well

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-6.svg)

7. Holding the center firmly, reach underneath and pull each corner flap upward to form a petal

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-7.svg)

8. Pull up the next layer of flaps between the petals to create a second row of petals

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-8.svg)

9. Gently pull up the final layer to form the outermost petals

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-9.svg)

10. For the base, fold the green sheet the same way (steps 1-5) and place the flower on top

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-10.svg)

11. Pull the green flaps outward to create the lily pad leaves beneath the flower

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/lotus/step-11.svg)


## Tips

- **Hold firmly**: Keep pressure on the center when pulling petals up to prevent the model from unfolding
- **Gentle pulling**: Pull each petal up slowly and carefully to avoid tearing
- **Paper choice**: Slightly thinner paper makes the pulling step much easier
- **Color options**: White with a yellow center dot, or pink with gradient paper, both look beautiful

## Fun Fact

The lotus flower holds deep significance in many Asian cultures. In Buddhism, it symbolizes purity because it blooms beautifully from muddy water. In ancient Egypt, the lotus was associated with rebirth and the sun. The lotus is also the national flower of India and Vietnam.

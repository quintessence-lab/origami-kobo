## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface
- String and a small paper basket (optional, for decoration)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it diagonally both ways, then unfold

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-1.svg)

2. Fold in half horizontally and vertically to create creases, then unfold

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-2.svg)

3. Collapse along the creases into a square base (also called a waterbomb base)

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-3.svg)

4. With the open end pointing up, fold the left and right corners of the top layer to the center

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-4.svg)

5. Flip and repeat on the other side

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-5.svg)

6. Fold the left and right points of the top layer to the center

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-6.svg)

7. Flip and repeat on the other side

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-7.svg)

8. Fold the small triangles at the top down toward the center

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-8.svg)

9. Flip and repeat on the other side

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-9.svg)

10. Tuck each small triangle into the pocket beneath it

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-10.svg)

11. Flip and repeat the tucking on the other side

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-11.svg)

12. Find the small opening at the bottom and gently blow air into it to inflate the balloon

![Step 12](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-12.svg)

13. Shape the balloon by gently pressing the sides to round it out

![Step 13](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-13.svg)

14. Optionally, attach a small paper basket with string to complete the look

![Step 14](/images/origami/steps/hot-air-balloon/step-14.svg)


## Tips

- **Inflation**: Blow gently and steadily — too much force can unfold the tucked flaps
- **Symmetry**: Ensure each side is folded identically so the balloon inflates evenly
- **Decoration**: Use patterned paper for a colorful, festive balloon
- **Display**: Hang from a string attached to the ceiling for a floating effect

## Fun Fact

The first hot air balloon flight took place in France in 1783, carrying a sheep, a duck, and a rooster as passengers. The Montgolfier brothers who invented it got the idea by watching laundry float over a fire. This origami model is actually based on the traditional Japanese "waterbomb" — one of the oldest origami designs, dating back hundreds of years.

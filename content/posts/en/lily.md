## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm, white, pink, or orange recommended)
- A flat surface
- A pen or pencil (for curling petals)

## Instructions

1. Place the paper white side up and fold diagonally both ways, then unfold
2. Fold in half horizontally and vertically to create creases
3. Collapse along the creases into a square base (preliminary base)
4. Fold the top layer's left and right edges to meet the center crease
5. Fold the top triangle down, crease, and unfold
6. Lift the bottom point and petal fold upward, folding the sides inward
7. Repeat steps 4-6 on all four sides of the model
8. On each side, fold the upper flaps down so the model narrows
9. Fold the left and right edges of each face to the center line
10. Repeat on all four sides
11. Gently pull each of the four petals outward and downward
12. Curl each petal tip around a pen or pencil for a natural shape

## Tips

- **Petal curling**: Wrap each petal around a pen and hold for a few seconds to set the curve
- **Consistent folds**: Make sure the petal folds on all four sides are as identical as possible for symmetry
- **Stem**: Roll a green sheet of paper diagonally to create a stem and insert it into the base
- **Paper choice**: Slightly thicker paper holds the petal curls better

## Fun Fact

The lily is one of the classic origami flowers and shares the same base as the crane (the bird base). Lilies have been cultivated for over 3,000 years and appear in art and mythology around the world. In Japan, the yuri (lily) symbolizes purity, and the country is home to several beautiful native lily species.

## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper or A4 paper
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Fold the paper in half vertically, crease, and unfold

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-1.svg)

2. Fold the top left and right corners to meet the center crease

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-2.svg)

3. Fold the angled edges to the center crease again

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-3.svg)

4. Mountain fold the entire piece along the center

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-4.svg)

5. Fold one wing down (about 1-2cm from the bottom edge)

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-5.svg)

6. Flip and fold the other wing to match

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-6.svg)

7. Spread the wings horizontally and you're done!

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/airplane/step-7.svg)


## Tips

- **Symmetry**: Keeping the wings even is the key to straight flight
- **Nose weight**: A small piece of tape on the nose adds stability
- **Wing angle**: Bending the trailing edge of the wings up slightly increases flight time
- **Throwing technique**: Don't throw too hard — a smooth, snapping motion works best

## Fun Fact

The world record for paper airplane distance is 69.14 meters (set in 2012). In Japan, there are annual paper airplane competitions where participants compete for both distance and hang time.

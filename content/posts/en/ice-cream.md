## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper for the scoop (any color you like, 15cm × 15cm)
- 1 sheet of origami paper for the cone (brown or tan, 15cm × 15cm)
- Glue stick
- A flat surface

## Instructions (Cone)

1. Place the brown paper color side down and fold it into a triangle
2. Fold the left corner up to the top point
3. Fold the right corner up to the top point
4. Fold the top front flap down about halfway
5. Tuck the remaining top flap behind the model
6. You now have a cone shape with an opening at the top

## Instructions (Scoop)

1. Fold the paper into a triangle
2. Fold the left and right corners slightly inward
3. Fold the top point down about 1cm
4. Fold the bottom point up to create a rounded scoop shape
5. Flip over — this is the front of your ice cream scoop

## Assembly

Apply glue to the inside top of the cone and insert the bottom of the scoop into the opening.

## Tips

- **Flavor colors**: Use pink for strawberry, light brown for chocolate, yellow for vanilla, green for matcha
- **Toppings**: Draw sprinkles, a cherry, or chocolate drizzle with markers
- **Double scoop**: Make two scoops in different colors and stack them
- **Waffle pattern**: Draw a criss-cross pattern on the cone with a brown marker

## Fun Fact

The ice cream cone was popularized at the 1904 World's Fair in St. Louis. When an ice cream vendor ran out of dishes, a nearby waffle maker rolled his waffles into cones to help out. Today, the average person eats about 23 liters of ice cream per year.

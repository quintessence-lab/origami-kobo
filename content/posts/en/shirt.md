## What You Need

- 1 sheet of origami paper (15cm × 15cm)
- A flat surface

## Instructions

1. Place the paper colored side down and fold it in half vertically, then unfold

![Step 1](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-1.svg)

2. Fold the left and right edges to meet the center crease

![Step 2](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-2.svg)

3. Flip the paper over

![Step 3](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-3.svg)

4. Fold the top edge down about 1cm to create the collar band

![Step 4](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-4.svg)

5. Flip the paper over again

![Step 5](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-5.svg)

6. Fold the top-left and top-right corners diagonally down from the center to form the collar points

![Step 6](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-6.svg)

7. Fold the bottom edge up about one-third of the way

![Step 7](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-7.svg)

8. Unfold the bottom fold slightly and pull the inner edges outward to form the sleeves

![Step 8](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-8.svg)

9. Re-fold the bottom section upward

![Step 9](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-9.svg)

10. Tuck the bottom edge underneath the collar

![Step 10](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-10.svg)

11. Press everything flat and adjust the collar and sleeves

![Step 11](/images/origami/steps/shirt/step-11.svg)


## Tips

- **Collar angle**: Adjusting the angle of the collar folds changes the shirt style — wide for casual, narrow for formal
- **Sleeves**: Pulling the corners out evenly in step 8 creates symmetrical sleeves
- **Tie addition**: Fold a tiny triangle from a contrasting paper and slip it under the collar for a necktie
- **Gift card**: Glue the shirt onto folded cardstock to make a unique greeting card

## Fun Fact

The origami shirt is one of the most popular Father's Day crafts worldwide. In Japan, Father's Day (celebrated on the third Sunday of June) often features handmade gifts from children. This origami design became widely popular in the 1980s and has since inspired variations including pants, dresses, and entire paper wardrobes!
